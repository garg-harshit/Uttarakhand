Tradition & Culture Of Uttarakhand

COSTUMES OF KUMAUNI WOMEN

The women in the Kumaon region used to be found wearing a
Ghagra along with kameez (shirt) as a blouse. Today
Kumaoni women wear Saari along with a kameez (shit)
as a blouse. Kumaoni women also wear Pichoras. In the
Kumaon region, married women wear big Naths made from
gold covering their entire cheek, Hansuli, black beaded
Necklace or Chareu, Bichuye (toe rings) made from
silver, and sindoor etc.

COSTUMES OF GARHWALI WOMEN

In the Garhwal region of this Northern state, women
usually wear sari tied in a particular way, the pallu
going from the front and knotted on the shoulder,
with a waistband made of cloth.A married woman
was supposed to wear hansuli (silver ornament)
worn around the neck, guloband (resembles the
contemporary choker), black beads and silver
necklace called chareu, silver payal, silver
necklace, silver dhagula (bracelet) and bichuye
(toe rings).

NANDA DEVI RAJ JAT YATRA

The three-week-long Nanda Devi Raj Jat is a pilgrimage
and festival of Uttarakhand. The Raj-Jaat is celebrated
in Chamoli Garhwal districts, and traditionally only the
deities of Garhwal Division took part in it.
Sometimes, during the peaceful periods between the
Kingdoms of Garhwal and Kumaun, the Goddess "Nanda of
Almora" was invited and took part in the Raj-Jaat, while
mostly there were separate Nanda-Sunanda fairs in Kumaun.

KHADI HOLI

Khadi Holi, (literally Standing Holi) It is mostly
celebrated in the rural areas of Kumaon. The songs
of the Khari Holi are sung by the people, sporting
traditional white nokdaar topi, churidar payajama and
kurta, dance in groups to the tune of ethnic musical
instruments like the dhol, joda (metal instrument),
and Hurka. Men or women in groups singing Khadi
holi songs which are much more Kumaoni in flavour.
These groups of men are called tolis.

AIPAN ART

Aipan is an established-ritualistic folk art originating
from Kumaon in the Indian Himalayas. The art is done
mainly during special occasions, household ceremonies
and rituals. Practitioners believe that it invokes a
divine power which brings about good fortune and deters
evil. The art is special as it is done on empty walls,
which are brick-red in color, called Geru. The actual
art is done with a white paste made of rice flour.

JHORA

Jhora is one of the famous folk dances of Kumaon region
of Uttarakhand. It is basically a dance form which is
performed by the people belonging to both the high
and low caste. This is a community dance form where
all the barriers related to the caste system are thrown
to the wind. This dance form is performed with the
accompaniment of songs.

JAGAR

Jagar is a ritualized form of ancestor spirit worship
which is practiced in the hills of Uttarakhand, both in
Kumaon and Garhwal.As a ritual, Jagar is a way in which
gods and local deities are woken from their dormant stage
and asked for favors or remedies. The ritual is connected
to the idea of divine justice and is practiced to seek
penance for a crime or to seek justice from the gods for
some injustice. The word Jagar comes from the Sanskrit
root, Jaga, meaning "to wake".
